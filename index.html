Consumer fintech · Generative AI

SmartCart

Problem

Shoppers have price information but lack neutral, contextual judgement at the moment of purchase.

User

Budget-conscious and impulse-prone shoppers deciding whether a purchase fits their budget and goals.

Key decision

Return an explained Buy / Wait / Don't buy verdict—not another score or affiliate recommendation.

Trade-off

Anonymous per-device use lowers activation friction but gives up cross-device sync. Price scraping degrades to manual entry when retailers block it.

AI role

A Groq-hosted LLM generates structured verdict reasoning from the user's budget and goals. AI is central because explanation—not calculation alone—is the product value.

Live PWA with wallet model, savings goals, watchlist, reminders and price tracking. Adoption metrics are not documented.

04HOW I OPERATE

Operating model

01 · OBSERVE

Find repeated friction

Start with a real behaviour or operational gap: purchase regret, team imbalance, unsafe capital allocation or a rule students keep calculating by hand.

02 · FRAME

Write the decision

Define the user, problem hypothesis, intended behaviour change, success criteria and smallest useful scope before polishing an interface.

03 · CHOOSE

Select the mechanism

Use an LLM for contextual reasoning or summarisation, on-device ML for private verification, and deterministic logic where predictability matters more.

04 · SHIP

Build the proof

Turn the decision into a testable artifact: working flow, live deployment and full PRD. Scope around constraints instead of hiding them.

05 · LEARN

Keep unknowns visible

Separate shipped functionality from measured impact. If adoption or outcome data is not documented yet, say so rather than manufacturing proof.

OUTPUT

Decision quality

The result is not “more AI.” It is a clearer user decision, a defensible trade-off and an artifact that lets the next conversation become more specific.
